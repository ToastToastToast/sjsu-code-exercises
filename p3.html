Exercise 5

My bit project has a total of 11 different javascript functions to display the 5 arrays. This exercise was a group exercise so 3 of the arrays are created by group members listed in the sketch, Irvana Gottwald and Aakash Kumar. Nested for loops call on either numbers or text in an array and display different visuals depending on what variable is being called. For example, the number 4 calls a dark orange color that is displayed as a square or a pixel. The image arrays use minecraft images to display the first 2 arrays depicing a minecraft wolf and a Labrador. The arrays are brought together to create two scenes. The first being a haunted scene with the game AmongUs character chasing Link from Nintendo. The second scene is the population of dogs in a heavenly cloud environment praying to the chicken.

Project 3

Project 3 is a personal clock that displays hours, minutes, and seconds. The hours are displayed by the different trains that arrive at the train station. The black display box hanging off the station displays the next coming train. The key for what every symbol represents can be found on the left. The lightrail is the transportation I use to get to San Jose State Universitiy's campus. I've had bad memories of missing the train or worrying about the train's time schedule changing. The lightrail has played a big part of my school life experience. The acorns falling from the tree represent the minutes and will fall in groups of 10. This connects to my dog who barks every day at my oak tree at the squirrels grabbing acorns off the ground. When the minutes are up, a flock of birds will rush by with clouds of dust. This acts as a transition for the acorns to move back up into the tree. My dog loves to also chase birds too. The seconds are represented by the dog barking every second. My 1 year old dog barks way too much for everything.

The group project is a class project where groups of students gather images of numbers and use them to create a representational digital clock. My group was responsible for gathering the images for hours 12am-3am, 1-10 minutes, and 1-10 seconds. The images my group did are based on tacos as our ideas lead to resturants like taco bell being open really late at night.
